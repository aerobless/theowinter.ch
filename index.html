Work

I am a Lead Software Architect at Zühlke, focused on shaping software systems that can be understood, changed, and operated by real teams.

A lot of my recent work is around AI in software delivery: using agents and language models to support analysis, architecture, implementation, testing, documentation, and review without losing human judgement.

My bias is toward useful, boring-in-the-best-way systems: clear context, explicit tradeoffs, tight feedback loops, and tooling that makes the right thing easier to do.

Personal projects

Outside client work, I like making practical software for my own routines: home automation, personal agents, location history, and a second brain that stays useful.

Nullpaw is my personal chat agent. It runs on my home server, works through Telegram, and can use a practical toolbox: calendar, email, files, shell commands, note-taking, and memory across conversations.

Thereabout is a self-hosted alternative to Google Location History. It turns location data into a browsable timeline and heatmap, and it can run locally or on a server.
